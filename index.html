Words from the creator

In "Binding" project, I curated three different writings to reflect on how our ideas about technology and the spaces we live in have changed over time. Marinetti’s "The Futurist Manifesto" celebrates machines as powerful tools that can push society forward. On the other hand, McGuirk's "Honeywell, I’m Home!" echoed at how technology has quietly entered our homes and begun to shape daily life urging the digital surveillance. Last but not least, "The Good Room" by Frank Chimero asks how we can create digital and physical spaces that feel supportive, thoughtful, and truly good for people. It reflects on how design can acknowledge both the momentum of technological change and the need to build spaces that speak to us. Not just surround us.
