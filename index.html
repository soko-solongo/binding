Introduction

In "Binding" project, I curated three different writings to reflect on how our ideas about technology and the spaces we live in have changed over time. Marinetti’s Futurist Manifesto celebrates machines as powerful tools that can push society forward. On the other hand, McGuirk's Honeywell, I’m Home! echoed at how technology has quietly entered our homes and begun to shape daily life urging the digital surveillance. Last but not least, The Good Room by Frank Chimero asks how we can create digital and physical spaces that feel supportive, thoughtful, and truly good for people.

My project aims to balance the speed of new technology with design choices that keep human needs at the center. It reflects on how design can acknowledge both the momentum of technological change and the need to build spaces that speak to us. Not just surround us.
